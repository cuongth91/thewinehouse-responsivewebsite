Tasting notes from the wine's maker

About the wine

Charlotee Richard is a crisp and refreshing Sauvignon Blanc that boasts a pale straw color with green highlights. On the nose, it delivers aromas of zesty citrus and tropical fruit, such as passionfruit and pineapple, alongside hints of grass and herbs. On the palate, it offers flavors of juicy grapefruit and lime, balanced with subtle minerality and a bright acidity that leads to a clean and refreshing finish. Enjoy Charlotee Richard chilled as an aperitif or pair it with seafood dishes, light salads, or grilled vegetables.

When to drink

Enjoy now after decanting for 45 minutes or cellar through 2038.

Best dishes to match

Seasonal dishes that pair beautifully with Charlotee Richard:

  • Fresh seafood, such as oysters, shrimp, or grilled fish.
  • Light salads with citrus-based dressings.
  • Grilled vegetables, such as asparagus, zucchini, or bell peppers.
  • Soft and creamy cheeses, such as goat cheese or brie
  • Sushi or sashimi.
  • Light pasta dishes with olive oil, garlic, and herbs

Pairing Suggestion

Grilled Ribeye Steak with Herb Butter

Ingredients:

  • 2 Ribeye Steaks, about 1 pound each
  • 1 tablespoon of Olive Oil
  • Salt and Pepper
  • 4 tablespoons of Unsalted Butter, softened
  • 1 Garlic Clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of Fresh Parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of Fresh Rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of Fresh Thyme, chopped

How To Cook:

The robust flavors of a red wine pair well with a juicy and flavorful steak. Season the ribeye with salt and pepper, grill to your desired doneness, and top with a homemade herb butter made from fresh herbs, garlic, and butter.

Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

How To Cook:

The earthy flavors of mushrooms complement the subtle notes of cherry and spice in a red wine. Saut√© mushrooms in butter and garlic, then stir them into a creamy risotto made with arborio rice, chicken stock, and Parmesan cheese.

Pan-Seared Scallops with Lemon Garlic Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound sea scallops, patted dry
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

How To Cook:

The delicate sweetness of scallops pairs well with a light-bodied red wine. Season the scallops with salt and pepper, and sear them in butter until golden brown. Then, make a lemon garlic sauce with garlic, lemon juice, white wine, and butter.
